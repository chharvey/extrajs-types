Class Angle

A one-dimensional angular measurement.

In geometry, an angle is a pair of rays that share a vertex. This class encodes the measurement of such a figure.

An Angle is represented by a unitless number within the interval [0, 1), but can manifest in units such as degrees, radians, and turns. Angles can also be added, subtracted, and scaled.

congruent

  • congruent(angle: number | Angle): boolean
    • Defined in src/class/Angle.class.ts:320

    Return whether this Angle is congruent to the argument.

    Angles are congruent iff they have equal canons. Angles that are equal are also congruent.

    Equivalently, Angles are congruent iff their measures differ by a whole number of turns. For example, 225˚ and 945˚ are congruent, because their canons are both 225˚, and because they differ by 2 whole turns.

    Parameters

      the Angle to compare
